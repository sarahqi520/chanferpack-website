What Drives Packaging Line Pricing

1. Output Capacity (BPH)

The number of filling heads is the primary cost driver. A 6,000 BPH line needs 24 filling heads vs. 12 for a 3,000 BPH line — roughly doubling the filling module cost. This is why price doesn't scale linearly with capacity.

2. Automation Level

A semi-automatic line with manual loading costs 30–40% less than a fully automatic line with conveyor integration, automatic bottle unscramblers, and robotic palletizers. Most CHANFER standard lines are fully automatic.

3. Control System Tier

Siemens S7-1500 with 15" HMI and OEE dashboard costs $8,000–$12,000 more than a basic S7-1200 setup. For high-volume lines this is the right investment. For entry-level, the simpler PLC is sufficient.

4. Format Flexibility

A single-format dedicated line is cheapest. Each additional product format (different bottle size or cap) adds $1,500–$4,000 for change-parts (filling nozzles, guides, cap feeder bowls). A 5-format line can add $15,000–$25,000 vs. a single-format setup.

5. Liquid Type & Viscosity

Water-thin products (dishwash at 200 cP) use standard nozzles. High-viscosity concentrates (8,000–15,000 cP) need heated tanks, special nozzles, and flow meters — adding $5,000–$15,000. Corrosive chemicals may require PTFE-lined piping (+$3,000–$8,000).

6. Included Scope

The cheapest quotes often exclude: bottle unscrambler (saves $6,000–$12,000), induction sealer ($7,000–$11,000), shrink sleeve ($12,000–$22,000), carton packer ($22,000–$35,000). Always compare scope, not just headline price.

Pricing FAQ

How much does a 6,000 BPH detergent packaging line cost? +
A 6,000 BPH turnkey bottle filling line from China typically costs $85,000–$130,000 FOB. With freight ($6,000–$12,000), installation ($8,000–$15,000), and 6 months of spare parts (~$8,000), the total landed cost is $110,000–$165,000.
What is the cheapest detergent packaging line available? +
Entry-level 3,000 BPH bottle filling lines start at $48,000 FOB from China. These cover basic PET bottle formats (100ml–2L) with 12-head filling, standard screw capping, and self-adhesive labeling. A full 3,000 BPH project budget should be $65,000–$90,000 including freight and installation.
Why are Chinese packaging lines cheaper than European ones? +
Chinese manufacturers like CHANFER use the same PLC (Siemens, Mitsubishi) and pneumatic (Festo, SMC) brands as European competitors, but labor costs for fabrication and assembly are 60–70% lower. The result is equivalent engineering quality at 40–60% of the European price.
What hidden costs should I budget for a packaging line? +
Budget for: sea freight ($4,000–$20,000), import duties (0–10%), installation ($5,000–$20,000), training ($3,000–$8,000), spare parts for year 1 (5–8% of line value), and factory preparation (electrical, air, water). Total landed + setup cost is typically 30–50% above the FOB price.
How long does delivery take from China? +
Production: 6–12 weeks. Sea freight: 14–28 days to Africa/Middle East, 25–40 days to Latin America. Customs: 5–15 days. Installation: 7–14 days. Total: 3–5 months from order to production start.
Can I get a firm price without visiting the factory? +
Yes. CHANFER provides firm quotations within 48 hours based on your product specs (viscosity, bottle size, cap type, output target) and factory layout photo. Prices are valid for 30 days. A factory video tour is offered as an alternative to a physical visit.
