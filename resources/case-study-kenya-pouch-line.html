Case Study: Kenyan Eco-Detergent Brand Switches to Spout Pouches

Compliance with Kenya's 2020 single-use plastic ban drove a packaging pivot — and saved 60% on packaging material cost.

Location
Nairobi, Kenya
Industry
Eco Laundry Detergent
Capacity
2,000 PPH
Project Year
2025

The Customer

A Nairobi-based eco-friendly detergent startup founded in 2018, marketing plant-based laundry and dishwashing detergents to East Africa's growing middle class. The brand differentiates on 100% biodegradable formulas and recyclable packaging — a positioning that aligns with Kenya's strict environmental regulations.

Trigger event: Kenya's 2020 ban on single-use plastic bags expanded in 2024 to include HDPE/PET detergent bottles in retail under 5L. The brand had 18 months to transition or exit the retail market.

The Decision: Spout Pouches vs. Cartons

The brand evaluated 3 alternative packaging formats before choosing spout pouches:

Format Material cost/unit Plastic reduction Customer feedback
HDPE bottle (1L, old format) $0.18 0% (baseline) Familiar, but banned
Spout pouch (1L, new) $0.07 -72% Modern, eco-friendly
Paper carton (1L) $0.22 -85% Premium feel, but leaks
Stand-up pouch (1L) $0.05 -78% Cheapest, but hard to pour

The brand chose spout pouches for the balance of cost ($0.07), sustainability (72% plastic reduction), and consumer convenience (rigid spout is easy to pour and reseal).

The Solution

CHANFER designed a 2,000 PPH spout pouch line with the following configuration:

  • Spout inserter: Auto-load pre-cut spouts into pouch headers (Servo-driven, 30 spouts/min)
  • 8-station rotary filler: Volumetric pump + 2.5% accuracy, CIP-ready
  • Pouch former & sealer: Rollstock film feeding + 4-side seal + spout heat seal
  • Leak tester: 100% inline pressure test, auto-reject defective pouches
  • Carton erector & packer: 12 cartons/min, 6 pouches per carton

Total project cost (landed): $295,000 USD (FOB $220,000 + freight $18,000 + duties $22,000 + installation $25,000 + spares $10,000).

Challenges Overcome

1. Spout supply chain gap

Custom spouts (12mm diameter, 30mm length) had to be sourced from a specialty supplier in Mumbai, India. CHANFER coordinated with the Indian supplier on tooling design + 6-week lead time. First shipment arrived 2 weeks before line commissioning — tight but workable.

2. Nairobi altitude affecting pneumatic systems

Nairobi sits at 1,795m elevation. Standard pneumatic systems lose 15% efficiency above 1,500m. CHANFER specified altitude-compensated Festo cylinders. Result: full 2,000 PPH throughput maintained.

3. Heat-seal temperature drift

Local film batches varied in thickness by 8-12 microns, causing inconsistent seals. CHANFER installed an auto-temperature compensation system that adjusts seal temperature based on film thickness sensor data. Seal success rate: 99.5% in steady state.

Key Lessons

  1. Regulatory pressure can become a competitive advantage. The brand that pivoted fastest captured market share from slower competitors.
  2. Altitude matters for pneumatics. If your factory is above 1,500m (East Africa, Andean region), specify altitude compensation or lose 15% throughput.
  3. Custom spouts need 6-week lead time. Start sourcing spouts in parallel with line procurement, not sequentially.
  4. Spout pouches are the best balance of cost, sustainability, and consumer convenience for liquid detergent refills.
