How to Choose a Detergent Packaging Line Manufacturer

A 5-step decision framework used by 200+ global detergent brands — plus 12 red flags that save you from costly mistakes.

📩 Get Free Vendor Comparison 📋 Download Checklist (PDF)

Choosing the right liquid detergent packaging line manufacturer is a 7-figure decision that will affect your factory's productivity for 15–20 years. This guide distills 14 years of experience from CHANFER and our 200+ global installations into a practical decision framework.

Step 1 — Define Your Requirements Clearly

Before contacting any vendor, document these 7 parameters. Vendors who don't ask for them are red flag #1.

  1. Container type: Bottle (PET/HDPE), pouch (stand-up/spout), drum (jerry can/pail), or all three?
  2. Container sizes: Range (e.g., 500ml–5L) and primary size (80% of production)
  3. Production capacity: Bottles/hour or tons/day. Have a primary target and a stretch target.
  4. Product viscosity: Watery (1–100 cP), medium (100–1,000 cP), or thick (1,000–5,000 cP)
  5. Automation level: Semi-automatic (1–2 operators), fully automatic (1 operator + 1 supervisor), or lights-out (no operators)
  6. Factory conditions: Available floor space, ceiling height, power supply (380V/50Hz vs 220V/60Hz), compressed air capacity
  7. Certifications needed: CE, UL/CSA, ATEX, food-grade (FDA/EU), GMP, or local standards

Step 3 — Verify Technical Capability (Critical!)

After shortlisting 3–5 vendors, conduct these 4 verifications:

  • Factory audit (in-person or video): Verify production floor, welding quality, electrical cabinet assembly, testing bay.
  • Sample testing: Send your actual product and 1,000 containers for a factory acceptance test (FAT). This costs USD 2,000–5,000 but saves you from a 6-figure mistake.
  • Reference calls: Speak with at least 2 existing customers who bought a similar line 1–3 years ago. Ask about delivery time, commissioning quality, and after-sales responsiveness.
  • Bank & legal check: Verify the company's business license, export records, and any lawsuits. Use services like Panjiva, ImportGenius, or local credit agencies.

Step 5 — Visit the Factory (Final Check)

Always visit the shortlisted manufacturer's factory before signing. If travel isn't possible, request a live video tour and have a third-party inspector (SGS, BV, TÜV) visit on your behalf (USD 2,000–4,000).

12 Red Flags to Avoid

❌ Quote is 30%+ below market average (likely cheap components or hidden costs)
❌ No physical factory address or refuses factory visits
❌ No reference customers in your region or industry
❌ Cannot provide material certificates (304 vs 316 stainless steel)
❌ Warranty less than 12 months or excludes critical components
❌ No clear after-sales contact or service SLA
❌ Pushes you to pay 100% upfront before manufacturing
❌ Cannot explain their quality control process
❌ Quotations lack detailed itemization (single lump sum)
❌ No CE/UL/ISO certificates (counterfeits are common)
❌ Subcontracts 100% of manufacturing (trading company, not manufacturer)
❌ Refuses to provide a sample test or pilot run

5 Questions to Ask Every Vendor

  1. "How many lines of this exact configuration have you delivered in the past 3 years?" (Look for 5+ for credibility)
  2. "What is your spare parts delivery time for a critical component failure?" (24–48 hours is industry standard)
  3. "Can you provide a live video tour of your factory right now?" (Refusal = red flag)
  4. "What is included vs. excluded in your warranty?" (Get this in writing)
  5. "Can I speak with 2 of your customers who bought a similar line?" (If they refuse, walk away)
