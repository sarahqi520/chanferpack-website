Detergent Packaging Line Cost: 2026 Pricing Guide

Practical guide for detergent brand owners and procurement managers

If you're budgeting for a new liquid detergent packaging line, the headline price tag is only 30% of what you'll actually spend. Hidden costs—installation, training, spare parts, utilities, and certification—can add 50-80% to the initial quote. This guide breaks down the real total cost of ownership (TCO) for bottle, pouch, and drum filling lines from a 200-9,000 USD for entry-level machines to turnkey plants exceeding 1 million USD.

5-Step Decision Framework

Step 1: Identify production capacity

Calculate required bottles per hour (BPH) and shifts per day.

Step 2: Choose container format

Select bottle, pouch, or drum format based on market and product viscosity.

Step 3: Determine automation level

Decide between semi-automatic and fully automatic line based on labor costs and target output.

Step 4: Budget for hidden costs

Add 50-80% of equipment cost for installation, training, utilities, certification, and spare parts.

Step 5: Compare TCO, not just price

Evaluate 5-year total cost of ownership: energy, maintenance, downtime, and after-sales support.
